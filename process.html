Process & Approach

How I think, how I work.

Good outcomes aren't a coincidence. They're the result of a thoughtful process applied consistently — and adapted when it needs to be.

01

The process

01 Discovery

Understand before anything else.

Every engagement starts with understanding — not assumptions. I get into the business goals, the user context, the competitive landscape and the existing product. I ask a lot of questions. I listen more than I talk. I read support tickets, talk to users, shadow sales calls and review analytics before I open a design tool.

User interviews Stakeholder mapping Analytics review Competitive audit Heuristic evaluation
02 Define

Frame the right problem.

Most design problems aren't what they appear to be on the surface. Before moving into solutions, I define the core problem clearly and align the team around it. This is where I turn messy research into a clear direction — a problem statement, principles for the solution, and a shared understanding of what success looks like.

Problem framing Journey mapping How Might We Design principles Success metrics
03 Explore

Make ideas tangible, quickly.

I explore widely before committing. Low-fidelity concepts, whiteboarding sessions with the team, parallel alternatives. The goal at this stage is to challenge assumptions and surface options — not to polish a single idea that might be wrong. I share rough work early and often.

Sketching Wireframes Concept explorations Workshops Rapid prototyping
04 Design

Craft the solution with intent.

Once the direction is clear, I move into high-fidelity design — but craft doesn't mean perfection, it means deliberate. Every decision is made for a reason. Spacing, hierarchy, motion, colour — all intentional. I design with engineering feasibility in mind, and I stay close to developers throughout.

Figma Design systems Interaction design Responsive layouts Motion design
05 Test & Refine

Validate with real people.

Designs aren't finished until they've been tested with users. I run moderated usability sessions, gather feedback, identify where assumptions broke down and iterate fast. I'm not precious about my work — if something isn't working, it changes.

Usability testing A/B testing Prototype testing Feedback synthesis
06 Deliver

Ship, then stay close.

Handoff isn't the end. I provide detailed developer specs, stay available during build, review implementations and catch issues before they reach users. After launch, I track the metrics that matter and feed learnings back into the next iteration.

Dev handoff QA review Launch support Post-launch metrics Iteration planning

03

Working with me

What you can expect.

  • Clear communication throughout — no black boxes or surprise reveals.
  • Honest feedback, even when it's not what you hoped to hear.
  • Work that's grounded in user needs, not just visual taste.
  • Collaboration that makes your team better, not dependent.
  • Deliverables that engineering can actually build without constant hand-holding.
  • A design partner who understands product — not just a pair of hands.

Engagement types.

Project-based

Defined scope, clear outcome. Good for specific product challenges, redesigns or new feature areas.

Embedded / retainer

Ongoing design partner, integrated with your team. Good for startups scaling their product function.

Advisory / review

Design critique, strategy sessions, process setup. Good for teams with designers who want a senior perspective.
