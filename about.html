Who I am

I make complex digital products feel simple.

I'm Bobby Anderson — a senior product designer and founder of This is RAD, with over 20 years of experience working across fintech, banking, SaaS, health tech and consumer products. My work sits at the intersection of UX, UI and product strategy — where the real design decisions happen.

I'm not just interested in how things look. I'm interested in how they work, why they exist, and whether they're actually solving the right problem. I founded Robert Anderson Design Ltd in 2015, and have been operating independently ever since — working with everyone from early-stage startups to global financial institutions.

My approach

Process over preference.

I believe good design is built on rigorous thinking, honest conversations and a genuine curiosity about the people using the product. I push back when something doesn't make sense. I ask the questions that others avoid. And I care deeply about getting things right.

That said — I ship. I don't disappear into research loops. I work iteratively, keep stakeholders close, and move with purpose.

What I value

Clarity over cleverness

A design that requires explanation has already failed. Simplicity is earned, not assumed.

Opinions with humility

I have strong views — but I hold them loosely. Good ideas can come from anywhere.

Craft in the details

The small things matter. Spacing, hierarchy, motion — these signal standards across the product.

Speed without shortcuts

Moving fast is important. So is doing it without leaving invisible debt for others to unpick later.

Who I enjoy working with

Ambitious teams with high standards.

I do my best work with founders and product teams who care deeply about what they're building — who treat design as a strategic function, not a finishing step. Companies that are genuinely trying to build something that makes a dent.

If you've got a vague brief and an open mind, I'm probably the right person. If you need someone who'll execute a fully-formed spec without question, I'm probably not.

What retainer work means in practice

Retained clients get a senior designer embedded in their team — not project-by-project thinking, but genuine continuity. I know the product, the brand standards, the stakeholders and the codebase. That context compounds over time and produces better outcomes than a fresh engagement every few months.

What agencies get from a design partner

When agencies bring me in, they get someone who operates at a senior level without needing to be managed. I can take a brief directly from a client, navigate stakeholder relationships, and deliver to a standard that reflects well on the agency. Trusted enough to be in the room, capable enough to lead the work.
