Featured · Product Thinking

Simplicity is a product decision, not a design preference.

When teams treat simplicity as an aesthetic preference — something a designer wants because they like clean things — they miss the point entirely. Simplicity is a product decision. It has business implications, user implications and organisational implications. Choosing to remove something from a product means someone in power was convinced it shouldn't be there. That's a hard conversation. It's also one of the most valuable ones a designer can initiate.

The products that feel genuinely simple aren't simple by accident. Someone fought for every removal.
