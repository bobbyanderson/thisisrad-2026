How I work with startups

Startups need a different kind of designer.

Enterprise design and startup design are not the same discipline. In large organisations, the challenge is usually navigating complexity — committees, compliance, legacy systems, competing stakeholders. In startups, the challenge is different: how do you make excellent decisions quickly, with incomplete information, and ship something that actually moves the needle?

I've worked with early-stage companies, funded startups and fast-scaling businesses across fintech, SaaS, e-commerce and consumer apps. The common thread isn't the sector — it's the mindset required. Fast, considered, pragmatic. Design that's good enough to ship and built well enough to grow on.

"We didn't want to cut features just to deliver on time. We wanted to build on them."

Case Study 01 — Money Dashboard

Taking a fintech web product native — ahead of the entire market.

Money Dashboard were Edinburgh's leading personal finance platform. Strong web product, loyal user base, genuine traction. After a successful funding round, they needed native iOS and Android apps — designed and ready in weeks, not months.

My role as Head of Design at xDesign was to lead the process: understanding what mattered to their users, making sharp decisions about what to carry over from the web experience versus what to rebuild for mobile, and delivering a complete design system on a timeline with no room for hesitation.

The easy answer would have been to shrink the web product down. We didn't take it. Tagged spending, savings goals, budget tracking — features that would become industry standard years later — were designed into the app from launch. We built toward where personal finance was heading, not toward what already existed.

Core app screens — spending overview, budget tracking and savings goals built for native mobile
Onboarding flow — designed for both new users and the existing web audience migrating to mobile

The App Store reviews after launch said more than any brief could. Money Dashboard went on to raise close to £5m in crowdfunding in the years that followed.

Case Study 02 — SaaS Platform (NDA)

Fixing the onboarding problem no one wanted to admit was there.

A B2B SaaS platform with a healthy signup rate and a stalled activation metric. The team assumed it was a marketing problem. The data told a different story once we looked at it properly — users were arriving, exploring briefly and leaving before they'd seen any real value. The issue wasn't acquisition. It was the first ten minutes.

Three weeks of session analysis and qualitative interviews identified three distinct friction points — none of which were obvious from the dashboard. We rebuilt the onboarding flow as a progressive value reveal: get users to their first meaningful outcome within two minutes, not fifteen. Reduce the cognitive load of setup. Show value before asking for commitment.

The result was a 38% improvement in activation rate in the first month post-launch, and a significant drop in support tickets raised during the onboarding window.

+38% activation rate ↓ Onboarding support tickets Faster time to first value
Case Study 03 — Scale-up (NDA)

A design system that gave 30 engineers a shared language.

A fast-growing startup with three product squads shipping UI from memory and assumption. Twelve button variants. Four different spacing scales. No token system. Designers were recreating components from scratch every sprint. Engineers were doing the same and assuming the other team had a canonical source. Neither did.

The work started with an audit — cataloguing every UI pattern in production before proposing anything new. From there, a token-first architecture with semantic naming, built in Figma and documented for engineering in parallel rather than handed over at the end.

The result: a shared component library the whole team could use confidently, significantly faster handoff, and a consistent product UI that scaled with the company rather than against it.

Consistent UI across product Faster design → engineering handoff Token-first · Figma + code
Why it works

Startups do better with a senior generalist than a team of juniors.

Early-stage companies often try to solve design by hiring cheap and wide. The result is a lot of output with unclear ownership and no consistent standard. A single senior designer who can research, design, prototype, test and implement — and who has done this across multiple products — produces better outcomes at lower overhead.

I'm not a team of one pretending to be an agency. I'm a senior practitioner who knows what they're good at, works well with founders and product teams, and cares about the quality of the outcome rather than the billable hours it takes to get there.
